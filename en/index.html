ABOUT

    Hi, my name is Gustavo Falcão, I'm 26 years old, and I was born in Pernambuco, Brazil. I am a Full-Stack Developer and self-taught student, studying Computer Engineering, in the city of Porto - Portugal, where I currently live. I like to use my free time looking for information about new technologies, scientific facts and working on projects.

    After starting my professional life in the Electronics area, I chose to dedicate myself to the maintenance of Computers and Smartphones/Tablets, where I improved my skills with soldering and maintenance of electronic components. I quickly developed a taste for solving Windows and Android software problems, which greatly improved my knowledge of these systems, it also led me to get to know Linux-based system distributions, with all their development and customization power. I started using Debian, where I learned a lot about its terminal, soon I was automating small tasks in Shell Script and getting to know other Linux distributions. So I started to study web and mobile development in a self-taught way and this was my entry into development, which soon brought me other curiosities and needs that led me to my academic path.

    Currently, I have knowledge in several technologies and Development and Designer tools, mainly HTML, CSS, JavaScript, NodeJS, ReactJS, UI/UX, Figma, Photoshop, Illustrator, CorelDRAW and GIMP. Also, I worked with several other languages and tools in small projects. My academic trajectory allowed me to learn several languages, tools and skills, such as C++, C#, Java, SQL, Python and managing Windows and Linux servers.

    With the end of my course, in Networks and Computer Systems, I soon started my academic trajectory in Computer Engineering, dedicating my free time to self-taught studies in web and mobile development. I like to seek new knowledge and apply this knowledge in my projects, in which I try to be as objective as possible, with characteristics of my daily life and with a modern and easy look. Working on these projects I run into real problems and solving those problems is the most important part of my way of learning. because, this is the process of “How I turn Problems into Knowledge”.
